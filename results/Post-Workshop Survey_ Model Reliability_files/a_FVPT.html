Model reliability

9 respondents. One bar per model, height = mean reliability on the survey's 1–7 scale (1 = least reliable, 7 = most reliable).

Reading the scale: the survey mixed word and number responses on the same 1–7 scale (1 = least reliable, 7 = most reliable) — "Reliable" was recorded as 7, "Unpredictable" as 1, and everyone else answered with a number (2, 3, or 5) directly. Averaged together, Fable and Sonnet read highest, Opus is mixed (one 7 alongside two 3s and a 2), and Haiku's single response was the lowest possible score.

Fable's "Reliable" rating is worth reading carefully: that same respondent's translation attempt didn't actually finish — it stopped after ~30 minutes when they ran out of credits — so "reliable" here describes what Claude did before it ran out, not a completed translation.

With 1–4 responses per model, treat this as a snapshot of these 9 attempts, not a reliability ranking between models.
